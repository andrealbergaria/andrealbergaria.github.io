Common divisors

Known Information

A composite, number, has at least two prime factors.

If they have 4 factors, two of them must be prime

End known information

The [], notation, implies that they are a list of divisors...

Example of numbers, that have 4 factors (including 1 and itself)

55 = [1, 5, 11, 55]

77 = [1, 7, 11, 77]

15 = [1,3,5,15]

If a divisor (say A) belongs, to a number (say B), the divisors of (A) are also divisors of B.

Example

12 = [1,2,3,6,12]

24 = [1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 8, 12, 24]

As we can see 24, has divisors 2,3,6 and 12, same as 12…

Strategy

We get divisors of a number, and if it includes other number, it includes all its divisors, including the prime factors. Example

55 = [1, 5, 11, 55]

110 = [1, 2, 5, 10, 11, 22, 55, 110]

Since 55 is divisor of 110, it has the same prime divisors as 55 (namely 5 and 11)

Just a observation, since 110 is even, it also has 2 as factor, and hence also 22...(divide 110 by 2)..
