GoldBach Conjecture

Every even integer greater than 2 can be written as the sum of two primes

Just using parity rules , to know which values are ok. So valid numbers are even+even, and odd+odd

So , we only want even+even, or odd+odd, because those are the ones that summed give even.

Suppose even number 12.

We start by,

12 = 11 + 1.

Then subtract 1 to 11, and add one to 1 ...we get

12 = 10 + 2

Subtract 1 to ten and add one to 2

12 = 9 + 3

12 = 8 + 4

12 = 7 + 5

and keep doing that, until we get

12 = 6 + 6 (the same summand..after this it repeats the sequences)

e.g, next sequence is,

12 = 5+ 7

It has already been verified.
